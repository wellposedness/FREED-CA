Six Cognitive Types (unchanged)

Two axes: loop depth (Builder → Navigator → Drifter) and grounding (Physics vs Symbol). All six types from v10 are preserved exactly.

Collective Memory Hypothesis

No individual cell has memory. Each cell's state is purely its current energy and type. Yet the population accumulates information about past environments through differential survival: perturbations that kill Symbol cells select toward Physics cells, and Physics cells are less vulnerable to subsequent perturbations.

The measurable prediction: mortality after perturbation N < mortality after perturbation 1. The distribution shifts. The ecosystem remembers what the cells cannot.

This is not learning. No cell updates a weight. It is selection pressure leaving a structural trace — information stored in the type histogram, not in any individual.

Resource Topology

Energy is no longer uniform. Six quasi-stable hotspots drift slowly across the field, each providing a local gain bonus to nearby cells. Hotspots make the foam heterogeneous — cells near a hotspot survive longer, creating spatial structure independent of the Mandelbrot bands.

Perturbation Response

Each shock rearranges the escape bands every cell reads. Cells that were predicting well suddenly face a different landscape. Symbol cells are hit hardest — their internal map lags behind the new reality.

The mortality bar chart below the Zipf histogram shows each shock's death toll. A downward trend is the signal: the population has shifted toward types that survive environmental discontinuity. The ecosystem has stored information about the pattern of past shocks.
