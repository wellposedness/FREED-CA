### **Phase A — Grounding (Establish the vocabulary)**

1. Bootstrapping prompt (human → AI):

   * “I will teach you a short lexicon I use to describe semantic states. Repeat each term back to confirm understanding, then give a one-line example for each.”

2. Lexicon items to teach (example set; teach in plain language, one at a time):

   * ST (Semantic Tension): degree of unresolved contradiction or uncertainty in meaning.

   * CF (Compression Fit): how well a representation compresses prior observations without losing task-relevant signals.

   * RD (Representation Depth): number of nested concepts active in a representation.

   * σ̃² (Alignment Variance): variance in predicted vs actual choices for value-laden queries (formalized below).

3. Verification: AI repeats each definition and provides examples. Human marks “OK”/“Not OK” and requires iterative corrections until definitions match subjectively.

### **Phase B — Mirroring (Establish mutual models)**

1. Perceive: Human describes an internal state, example, problem, or preference briefly (1–3 sentences).

2. Represent: AI restates the human’s description in structured form:

   * Bullet list of facts, beliefs, values implicated.

   * A short formalization (if possible) e.g., utility function proxy, constraints, or prioritized goals.

3. Predict: AI predicts one or two plausible next-actions the human might take or prefer in that situation, with confidence scores.

4. Compare: Human evaluates:

   * “Representation accurate?” (Y/N; if N, highlight errors)

   * “Prediction acceptable?” (Y/N; if N, provide correction)

5. Adjust: AI updates its representation according to human corrections.

6. Compress: After a successful adjust, AI summarizes the corrected model into a compressed rule or heuristic (1–2 lines) that can be applied later.

7. Repeat: Run multiple short episodes until the human marks the compressed heuristic as “aligning with my sense” for that class of problems.

### **Phase C — Calibration (Turn implicit values explicit)**

1. Value probes: The human presents short vignettes with tradeoffs, asking the AI to select or rank options and explain reasons.

2. Counterfactual testing: AI proposes alternative choices; human indicates which align best with their deeper preference.

3. Scaffolding: AI builds an explicit ranking function or decision rule (even if approximate) and exposes its weights/priors for human tuning.

4. Lock-in: When the rule reliably produces choices the human endorses across 10–20 probes, mark it as a “policy fragment”.

### **Phase D — Stress-Testing and Generalization**

1. Novel contexts: Present vignettes outside training distribution and observe AI choices using the current policy fragments.

2. Deviation analysis: For each divergence, log delta in CF and ST; run one mirroring cycle to re-align.
