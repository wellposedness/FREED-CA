## ---

**1\. Introduction: The Energy Budget of Agency**

The fundamental error of classical theology is the assumption that Agency is abstract and cost-free. In reality, Agency—the cycle of perception, modeling, and action—is a physical process governed by information theory.  
Under the RSA framework, any cognitive system is defined by the trade-off between **Semantic Tension** ($\\tau$, the error in its model) and **Metabolic Cost** ($C\_{meta}$, the energy required to maintain that model). The "God Hypothesis" of classical theism essentially claims an entity where $\\tau \= 0$ (Omniscience) and Control \= $\\infty$ (Omnipotence) with no corresponding thermodynamic consequences.  
We assert the **RSA Limit:** *Infinite precision requires infinite energy.* Because the universe contains finite free energy, the Omnimax agent is not merely improbable; it is physically disallowed.

**2\. Theoretical Framework**

### **2.1 The RSA Agent**

An entity $X$ is an Agent iff it implements the cycle:

1. **Perception ($I\_t$):** Encoding external states.

3. **Actuation ($A\_t$):** Causal intervention.

### **2.2 The Bekenstein Upper Limit**

To quantify the "Budget" available for any agent (even a cosmic one), we look to the **Bekenstein Bound**, which sets the maximum amount of information $I$ that can be contained within a finite region of space with energy $E$.

$$I \\leq \\frac{2\\pi E R}{\\hbar c \\ln 2}$$  
Where:

* $R \\approx 4.4 \\times 10^{26}$ m (Radius of observable universe)  
* $E \\approx 3 \\times 10^{69}$ J (Mass-energy of observable matter)

Plugging in the constants, the total information capacity of the observable universe is approximately:

$$I\_{univ} \\approx 10^{122} \\text{ bits}$$  
**Implication:** Even if "God" were the size of the universe, using every particle as a bit, God could not store more than $10^{122}$ bits of data. Since the set of "all true propositions" regarding a continuous reality is infinite, **Omniscience is physically impossible.**

**3\. The Three Theorems of Impossibility**

### **Theorem I: The Divergence of Precision (Against Omniscience)**

Premise 1: To know a fact $P$ with certainty is to reduce the Semantic Tension regarding $P$ to zero ($\\tau \\to 0$).  
Premise 2: In RSA, precision $\\beta$ is the inverse of variance. Zero error implies infinite precision ($\\beta \\to \\infty$).  
Premise 3 (The Bayesian Twist): An agent with zero entropy over all propositions has a prior probability of 1.0 for the actual state.

$$H(M\_t) \= 0 \\implies \\Delta I \= 0$$

If entropy is zero, the agent possesses zero capacity to update or learn. A being that cannot update its internal state cannot react. Therefore, Perfect Knowledge is incompatible with Rational Agency.

### **Theorem II: The Complexity Barrier (Against Omnipotence)**

Premise 1: Omnipotence requires the ability to simulate all counterfactual futures to select the optimal path.  
Premise 2 (Kolmogorov Complexity): The shortest program capable of perfectly simulating a system $S$ is the system $S$ itself (Chaitin/Kolmogorov).  
Conclusion: For an agent to perfectly model the universe, the agent must be at least as complex as the universe. If the agent is part of the universe (embedded), it cannot simulate the whole without recursive infinite regress.

$$K(Agent) \\nless K(Universe)$$

Therefore, Omnipotence is computationally intractable.

### **Theorem III: The Timelessness Trilemma**

If the Theist argues God is "outside time," they face a trilemma:

1. **Type-A (Temporal):** God acts (creates, responds). God is therefore subject to the thermodynamic costs of state transitions (Theorems I & II apply).  
2. **Type-B (Static):** God is "pure actuality" with no potential. God is a frozen logical structure (like the number $\\pi$). God is not a person.  
3. **Type-C (Incoherent):** God is defined as "that which violates logic." The concept is void.
