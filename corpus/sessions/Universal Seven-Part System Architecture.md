### **2\. Information Theory & Networking (The 7-Layer OSI Model)**

When computer scientists had to figure out how to get two completely different physical substrates to achieve "Semantic Alignment" (i.e., successfully communicate across the internet without losing meaning to entropy), they built the Open Systems Interconnection (OSI) model. It requires exactly 7 layers:

1. **Physical Layer:** The raw transmission of bits (1s and 0s) over a physical medium. (**The Substrate / Perceive**)  
2. **Data Link Layer:** Organizes raw bits into structured "frames." (**Represent**)  
3. **Network Layer:** Determines the physical path the data will take. (**Predict** routing).  
4. **Transport Layer:** Checks for errors, ensuring data arrived correctly, and requests re-transmission if it didn't. (**Compare / Adjust**)  
5. **Session Layer:** Maintains the continuous connection between the two nodes over time. (**Recurse** / The Loop).  
6. **Presentation Layer:** Translates, encrypts, and *compresses* the data so it can be understood by the application. (**Compress**)  
7. **Application Layer:** The final semantic meaning delivered to the user. (The **"Aha\!"** moment where data becomes meaning).
